Disease: Diabetes Mellitus
Description: Chronic metabolic disorder characterized by high blood sugar levels
Types: Type 1 (autoimmune), Type 2 (insulin resistance), Gestational
Key symptoms: Frequent urination, excessive thirst, fatigue, blurred vision, slow healing wounds
Risk factors: Obesity, family history, sedentary lifestyle, age over 45
Complications: Heart disease, kidney damage, nerve damage, vision problems
Management: Diet control, exercise, medication, regular blood sugar monitoring

Disease: Hypertension (High Blood Pressure)
Description: Persistently elevated blood pressure above 130/80 mmHg
Types: Primary (essential), Secondary (caused by other conditions)
Key symptoms: Often silent; headaches, dizziness, chest pain in severe cases
Risk factors: Age, family history, obesity, high salt diet, stress, smoking
Complications: Heart attack, stroke, kidney disease, vision loss
Management: Lifestyle changes, medication, regular BP monitoring

Disease: Common Cold
Description: Viral upper respiratory infection affecting nose and throat
Causes: Rhinovirus (most common), coronavirus, RSV
Key symptoms: Runny nose, sneezing, sore throat, mild cough, mild fever
Duration: Usually 7-10 days, self-limiting
Treatment: Rest, fluids, symptom relief (no antibiotics needed)
Prevention: Hand hygiene, avoid close contact with sick individuals

Disease: Influenza (Flu)
Description: Contagious respiratory illness caused by influenza viruses
Types: Influenza A, B, C (A and B cause seasonal epidemics)
Key symptoms: High fever, body aches, fatigue, headache, cough, sore throat
Duration: 1-2 weeks, can be severe in high-risk groups
Treatment: Rest, fluids, antivirals (if started within 48 hours)
Prevention: Annual flu vaccination, hand hygiene

Disease: COVID-19
Description: Respiratory illness caused by SARS-CoV-2 coronavirus
Key symptoms: Fever, dry cough, fatigue, loss of smell/taste, shortness of breath
Severity: Ranges from mild to severe/critical
Complications: Pneumonia, ARDS, multi-organ failure
Treatment: Supportive care, antivirals, oxygen therapy for severe cases
Prevention: Vaccination, masking, social distancing, hand hygiene

Disease: Migraine
Description: Neurological condition characterized by intense, recurring headaches
Types: With aura, without aura, chronic migraine
Key symptoms: Throbbing headache (usually one-sided), nausea, light/sound sensitivity
Triggers: Stress, hormones, certain foods, lack of sleep, weather changes
Duration: 4-72 hours
Treatment: Pain relievers, triptans, preventive medications

Disease: Asthma
Description: Chronic inflammatory disease of the airways
Key symptoms: Wheezing, shortness of breath, chest tightness, cough
Triggers: Allergens, exercise, cold air, smoke, respiratory infections
Classification: Intermittent, mild persistent, moderate persistent, severe persistent
Treatment: Rescue inhalers (bronchodilators), controller medications (steroids)
Management: Avoid triggers, use action plan, regular follow-up

Disease: Gastroenteritis
Description: Inflammation of the stomach and intestines, usually due to infection
Causes: Viral (norovirus, rotavirus), bacterial (Salmonella, E. coli), parasitic
Key symptoms: Diarrhea, vomiting, nausea, abdominal cramps, fever
Duration: 1-3 days (viral), longer for bacterial
Treatment: Oral rehydration, rest, avoid dairy, antiemetics if needed
When to seek help: Severe dehydration, bloody stool, high fever

Disease: Urinary Tract Infection (UTI)
Description: Infection in any part of the urinary system (bladder, urethra, kidneys)
Common in: Women more than men
Key symptoms: Burning urination, frequent urination, urgency, cloudy/bloody urine
Causes: Bacteria (usually E. coli) entering the urinary tract
Treatment: Antibiotics, increased fluid intake
Prevention: Good hygiene, adequate hydration, urinate after intercourse

Disease: Anemia
Description: Condition where blood lacks adequate healthy red blood cells
Types: Iron deficiency, vitamin B12 deficiency, chronic disease, hemolytic
Key symptoms: Fatigue, weakness, pale skin, dizziness, shortness of breath
Causes: Blood loss, poor diet, absorption problems, chronic diseases
Diagnosis: Blood tests (hemoglobin, hematocrit, iron studies)
Treatment: Depends on type - iron supplements, B12 injections, treat underlying cause

Disease: Dengue Fever
Description: Mosquito-borne viral infection common in tropical regions
Transmission: Aedes mosquito bites
Key symptoms: High fever, severe headache, eye pain, joint/muscle pain, rash
Warning signs: Abdominal pain, persistent vomiting, bleeding, lethargy
Duration: 2-7 days, recovery 1-2 weeks
Treatment: Supportive care, fluids, avoid NSAIDs (use paracetamol)
Prevention: Mosquito control, protective clothing, repellents

Disease: Malaria
Description: Parasitic disease transmitted through mosquito bites
Causes: Plasmodium parasites (P. falciparum most dangerous)
Key symptoms: Cyclic fever, chills, sweating, headache, body aches
Complications: Cerebral malaria, severe anemia, organ failure
Diagnosis: Blood smear, rapid diagnostic test
Treatment: Antimalarial drugs (artemisinin combinations)
Prevention: Mosquito nets, repellents, prophylactic medication in endemic areas

Disease: Anxiety Disorder
Description: Mental health condition characterized by excessive worry and fear
Types: Generalized anxiety, panic disorder, social anxiety, phobias
Key symptoms: Restlessness, rapid heartbeat, sweating, trembling, difficulty concentrating
Physical symptoms: Headaches, muscle tension, sleep problems, fatigue
Treatment: Therapy (CBT), medication (SSRIs, anxiolytics), lifestyle changes
Management: Stress management, exercise, adequate sleep, avoid caffeine/alcohol

Disease: Muscle Strain
Description: Overstretching or tearing of muscle fibers
Causes: Overuse, sudden movements, poor flexibility, fatigue
Grades: Grade 1 (mild), Grade 2 (moderate), Grade 3 (severe/complete tear)
Key symptoms: Pain, swelling, limited movement, muscle weakness
Treatment: RICE (Rest, Ice, Compression, Elevation), pain relievers
Recovery: Few days to several weeks depending on severity
Prevention: Warm-up, stretching, proper technique, adequate rest

Disease: Viral Infection (General)
Description: Illness caused by viruses affecting various body systems
Common types: Respiratory, gastrointestinal, skin infections
Key symptoms: Fever, fatigue, body aches, varies by virus type
Treatment: Usually supportive (rest, fluids); antivirals for specific viruses
Duration: Most resolve in 1-2 weeks
Prevention: Vaccination where available, good hygiene, avoid sick contacts
