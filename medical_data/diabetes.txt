Disease: Diabetes Mellitus

Definition:
Diabetes is a chronic disease that occurs when blood glucose levels are too high due to insufficient insulin production or insulin resistance.

Types:
1. Type 1 Diabetes – Autoimmune destruction of insulin-producing cells.
2. Type 2 Diabetes – Insulin resistance (most common).
3. Gestational Diabetes – Occurs during pregnancy.

Common Symptoms:
- Frequent urination
- Excessive thirst
- Fatigue
- Blurred vision
- Slow wound healing

Normal Lab Values:
- Fasting blood sugar: 70–99 mg/dL
- HbA1c: Below 5.7%

Diagnosis:
- Fasting glucose ≥ 126 mg/dL
- HbA1c ≥ 6.5%

Treatment:
- Lifestyle modification
- Metformin
- Insulin therapy
- Regular glucose monitoring

Complications:
- Kidney damage
- Nerve damage
- Heart disease
- Vision problems
